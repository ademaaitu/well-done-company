# Earthquake Safety Campaign — Strategic & Content Package

## Part 1. Strategic concept

### Target audience

- Adults in cities (home / mall / transport users)
- Mall staff and tenants
- Bus passengers and commuters
- Families with school-age children

### Campaign message

Fast, simple actions during and right after shaking save lives. Learn 3 core actions in under 60 seconds and practice them before an emergency.

### Main fear

Panic and not knowing what to do in the first critical seconds.

### Main trigger to action

A short self-check: “Would you make the right decision in the first 10 seconds?”

### How to stay convincing but not frightening

- Use actionable instructions instead of disaster visuals
- Use positive language: “Do this now” vs “Don’t panic”
- Show micro-success moments (correct behavior badges)
- Keep each instruction one sentence and one icon

### How to ensure understanding in 60 seconds

- Rule of 3: Drop, Cover, Hold On
- One scenario per location (home, mall, transport)
- Visual hierarchy: action first, explanation second
- End with one CTA: Scan QR and take 2-minute self-test

## Part 2. Content package

### 1) Video (1–1.5 min)

#### Storyboard (frame-by-frame)

1. Hook (0–5s): “Earthquake starts now. What do you do in 10 seconds?”
2. Scene A (home): person drops under table, protects head
3. Scene B (mall): person moves away from glass, follows signs calmly
4. Scene C (bus): passenger holds support, waits for safe stop
5. “Immediately after” block: check injuries, hazards, aftershock readiness
6. Final CTA: “Scan QR → Check your preparedness level”

#### Voice-over text

“In an earthquake, the first seconds matter. Drop, Cover, Hold On. Stay away from glass and unstable objects. In public spaces, follow emergency guidance calmly. After shaking stops, check for injuries and hazards, and prepare for aftershocks. Scan the QR code and test your readiness now.”

#### Visual approach

- High-contrast icons + short text overlays
- Minimal cinematic effects, clarity first
- Location labels: Home / Mall / Transport

#### Adaptation by channel

- Mall screens (mute): subtitles + icon-led instructions
- Buses: larger fonts, low-motion scenes for readability
- Social: 9:16 vertical, chapter-style cuts every 5–7s

#### CTA

“Take the 2-minute Earthquake Safety Test”

---

### 2) Poster (offline)

#### Headline

“Землетрясение: 3 шага, которые спасают жизнь”

#### Visual structure

- Top: headline + urgency line
- Center: 3 actions with icon blocks
- Bottom: QR + “Проверь себя за 2 минуты”

#### 3–5 key actions

- Drop, Cover, Hold On
- Stay away from glass / heavy shelves
- Follow official evacuation routes
- Check injuries and hazards after shaking
- Keep emergency contacts offline

#### QR destination concept

QR opens the interactive mini-module with personalized result and checklist.

#### Why noticed

- Strong contrast + large action verbs
- Minimum text, maximum visual anchors
- Real context labels: Home / Mall / Bus

#### Best placements

- Mall entrances and escalator areas
- Bus stop shelters, onboard screens
- Administrative service halls, schools, clinics

---

### 3) Longread (digital)

#### Structure

- Why this matters now
- During shaking: what to do immediately
- Right after shaking: first 5 minutes
- Typical mistakes and why they are dangerous
- Personal checklist

#### Content principles

- Short paragraphs (2–4 lines)
- One concept per block
- Plain language for mass audience
- Practical examples for home/mall/transport
